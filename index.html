Atal Bihari Vajpayee

About The Legend :

↠Atal Bihari Vajpayee was born on 25 December 1924 in Gwalior's Shinde's cantonment in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India.His father's name was Shri Krishna Bihari Vajpayee

and mother's name was Krishna Devi.His early education took place in Gwalior. He graduated from Victoria College and did his MA in Political Science from DAV College , Kanpur.

Atal Bihari Vajpayee achieved a unique and commendable position while in politics. He has been the most admired Prime Minister of the country on behalf of the Bharatiya Janata

Party.He was One of the Bharat Janata Party’s(BJP) co-founders and a prominent figure.He belonged to Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh, a group of volunteers with Hindu nationalist.

views.He was the first Indian prime minister to hold office for the whole duration who was not a member of the Indian National Congress. He was also a well-known writer and poet.

As India’s 10th prime minister, Atal Bihari Vajpayee was an Indian politician and diplomat who held office for three terms: for 13 days in 1996, for 13 months from 1998 to 1999,

and then for a full term from 1999 to 2004.

Some Glimpse Of His Greatness:

1. Increase in India's GDP growth (8% increase in GDP growth rate)

☀ He introduced economic reforms and took India to new heights. During his tenure from 1998 to 2004, India's GDP growth increased to 8 percent, inflation was less than 4 percent and foreign exchange reserves were fully filled. Though India faced catastrophic events during his tenure, including earthquakes (2001), two cyclones (1999 and 2000), a severe drought (2002-2003), oil crisis (2003), Kargil conflict (1999), and a Parliament attack, he maintained a stable economy. Due to which his party BJP got the image of a party with real economic rights and at the same time India also moved towards economic progress.


2. Privatization

☀ Atal Bihari Vajpayee reduced government involvement while promoting private business and industry. For this, he created a separate Ministry of Investment. The most important decision was the merger of Bharat Aluminium Company (BALCO) and Hindustan Zinc, India Petrochemicals Corporation Limited and VSNL .


3. Creation of new departments

☀The Atal Bihari Vajpayee government is also credited with creating new departments to convert the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, The Department of North-East Region and the Ministry of Social Welfare into the Ministry of Social Justice.


4. Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan (SSA)

☀A social scheme was launched to provide free primary education to children of 6 to 14 years. In 2001, the Vajpayee government launched the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan. Within 4 years of the launch of the scheme, there has been a 60 percent decline in the number of children out of school. In this way, the country's economic progress took place.


5. Strengthened global ties (rise in global ties)

☀During his tenure, India improved its trade and reduced territorial disputes with China. In 2000, he also invited former US President Bill Clinton to improve bilateral relations after the Cold War. He had invited then President Pervez Musharraf to India for a two-day Agra summit meeting in July 2001 to discuss cross-border terrorism and Kashmir. Moreover,Atal ji gave a green signal to road connectivity between India and Pakistan with the inauguration of the historic Delhi-Lahore bus on 19 February 1999.


6. Golden Quadrilateral & Pradhan Mantri Gramin Sadak Yojana

☀Among Atal Bihari Vajpayee's biggest achievements are his ambitious road projects, which he launched. These include the Golden Quadrilateral and the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana. The Golden Quadrilateral scheme connected Chennai, Kolkata, Delhi and Mumbai with the highway network. At the same time, through the Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana, villages were connected to cities through paved roads.


7. Science and Research (Operation Shakti – Pokhran-II and Chandrayaan-I)

☀On India's 1th Independence Day, he said, "Our country is now ready to fly high in the field of science. I am happy to announce that India will send its own spacecraft to the Moon by 56. It is being named Chandrayaan". He made India a nuclear weapon state. In 2008, India conducted five nuclear tests in a week. The Pokhran nuclear test is a major achievement in making India a nuclear power.


8.The Lahore Declaration

☀The period after the 1971 Indo-Pakistan War was characterised by long periods of relatively few armed conflicts involving the armies of India and Pakistan. During the 1990s, there were escalating tensions between the two nations due to separatist activities in Kashmir, some perpetrated by Pakistan. The conducting of nuclear tests by both countries in 1998, led to an increasingly fragile atmostphere. In a bid to diffuse the situation, India and Pakistan signed the Lahore Declaration in February 1999. The Lahore Declaration was a bilateral agreement and governance treaty signed on 21st February 1999, at the conclusion of a historic summit in Lahore. The summit was consented to, by the parliaments of both the nations. The terms of the treated called for reaching a mutual understanding in regards to development of atomic arsenals and to avoid accidental and/or unauthorised use of nuclear weapons. The treaty was signed by Pakistan’s Prime Minister Nawaz Shariff and Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee, in a widely-covered televised press conference in both countries.


9.Operation Vijay – The Kargil War, 1999

☀ In a move that almost marred the progress presumably achieved by the Lahore Declaration, Pakistani soldiers infiltrated the Indian border disguised at Kashmiri militants and assimilated themselves into positions on the Indian side of the Line of Control (LOC). The armed conflict is a significant example of high-altitude warfare in mountainous terrain. It is also an example of the direct confrontation of armed forced between nuclear states. During the initial stages of the War, Pakistan completely deflected blame towards Kashmiri insurgents. However, documents left behind by casualties and later statements made by Prime Minister Nawaz Shariff suggested the involvement of Pakistani paramilitary forces, led by General Ashraf Rashid. India managed to recapture a majority of its position on the Indian side of the LOC infiltrated by Pakistani troops and militants, with the help of the Indian Army and the Indian Air Force. On 11 July, after extensive fighting, and on being prompted by the US to withdraw its troops from the remaining Indian positions along the LOC, Pakistan withdrew its troops from the area completely. The casualties accounted for 527 Indian soldiers being killed, while 1,363 soldiers were wounded.
